FineVLA: Fine-Grained Instruction Alignment for Steerable Vision-Language-Action Policies

Xintong Hu*1, Xuhong Huang*1, Jinyu Zhang1, Yutong Yao1, Yuchong Sun2, Qiuyue Wang2, Mingsheng Li2, Sicheng Xie2, Yitao Liu1, Junhao Chen1, Yixuan Chen1, Yingming Zheng1, Shuai Bai2, Tao Yu†1

FineVLA builds a closed loop for action-instruction alignment, connecting fine-grained data construction, robotic video understanding, scalable annotation, and steerable VLA policy learning.

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models are moving beyond task-level robot control toward policies that can be steered by human instructions. However, existing robot datasets typically pair trajectories with coarse goal-level language, leaving action-relevant execution details—such as active arm, target object, approach direction, contact region, motion path, and final configuration—unspecified. This limits both policy learning and robotic video understanding, and the field still lacks scalable infrastructure, dedicated benchmarks, and training recipes for action-aligned fine-grained supervision.

We introduce FineVLA, a fully open-source framework for scaling fine-grained VLA data, robotic video understanding, and steerable VLA policies. FineVLA includes: (1) FineVLA-Tool, which unifies 972,247 trajectories across 85K tasks from 10 open-source datasets and constructs FineVLA-Data, a human-verified dataset of 47,159 fine-grained action-aligned trajectories; (2) RoboFine-Bench, a held-out benchmark with 500 videos, 10,816 atomic facts, and 1,030 VQA questions; (3) RoboFine-VLM, a robotics-specialized VLM for scalable annotation; and (4) FineVLA-Policy, a steerable policy trained with controlled mixtures of fine-grained and raw goal-level instructions.

Experiments show that fine-grained supervision improves policy learning without hurting goal-level success. In simulation, the best mixed setting reaches 86.8%/82.5% success on AlohaMix-OFT Easy/Hard. In real-world dual-arm manipulation, FG:Raw = 1:1 achieves 62.7/100 versus 49.9 for Raw-only and reduces instruction violations from 34% to 12%. On the held-out RoboFine-Bench, RoboFine-VLM achieves 71.0% VQA accuracy and 83.6% captioning score. We release the complete FineVLA suite to support future research on steerable VLA systems.

Method

FineVLA-Tool converts large-scale heterogeneous robot demonstrations into fine-grained, action-aligned instruction data through four stages:

  1. Data Collection & Format Conversion: Aggregate 972,247 trajectories from 10 open-source robot datasets into a unified LeRobot-style format.
  2. Canonicalization & Cleaning: Standardize action and state representations across embodiments, and remove corrupted or inconsistent trajectories.
  3. Clustering & Sampling: Use dynamic time warping (DTW) to identify representative trajectories, reducing redundancy while preserving manipulation diversity.
  4. Annotation Pipeline: Annotate selected trajectories with a ten-dimensional fine-grained schema covering action sequence, active actor, target object, contact & approach, trajectory & orientation, object interaction, failure & recovery, and body motion, followed by human verification.

FineVLA-Data & RoboFine-Bench

FineVLA-Data is a human-verified corpus of 47,159 fine-grained action-aligned trajectories drawn from 10 source datasets. Fine-grained annotations dramatically increase instruction information density: the average word count per trajectory rises from 9.3 to 96.8, an approximately 10.4× increase, while covering 47 unique action verbs across all sources.

RoboFine-Bench evaluates fine-grained robotic video understanding through complementary VQA and captioning tracks. The benchmark contains 500 videos from 10 robot datasets, covering 32 embodiments, diverse camera views, and a wide range of manipulation tasks. Each trajectory is paired with human-reviewed step-level annotations decomposed into 10,816 atomic facts across ten action-relevant dimensions, with 1,030 VQA questions.

Experiments & Results

RoboFine-Bench Results

RoboFine-VLM achieves the best performance among evaluated VLMs on RoboFine-Bench, reaching 71.0% VQA accuracy and 83.6% captioning score under the hard setting. Compared with its base model Qwen3.5-Plus, SFT on FineVLA-Data improves overall accuracy from 52.6% to 71.0%, with consistent gains across grounding, action, and state reasoning.

Simulation Results

In RoboTwin simulation, fine-grained supervision improves over Raw-only across all evaluated settings. The best setting, FG:Raw = 1:1, reaches 86.8%/82.5% on AlohaMix-OFT Easy/Hard, a gain of +15.0/+11.1 over the Raw-only baseline (71.8%/71.4%).

Real-World Steerability

In real-world dual-arm manipulation, the FG:Raw = 1:1 policy achieves 62.7/100 versus 49.9 for Raw-only and reduces instruction violations from 34% to 12%. Fine-grained supervision directly improves language-conditioned controllability on instruction-sensitive tasks such as approach direction, rotation direction, and active arm selection.

Key experimental results. Fine-grained and raw instructions are complementary, with the optimal FG:Raw ratio around 1:1 across simulation and real-world settings.
